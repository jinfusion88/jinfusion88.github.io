Part I

The Doctrine

The vocabulary I steered this whole project by.

JENGA

Linear, synergy dense towers that win fast and fall to one pulled block. Typically comes with Speed and a high ceiling, Typically highly fragile, in other words a glass cannon. My v1 ran four of them at once. Vadrok distilled the storm version.

SLOP

High floor piles of good stuff. Card quality over synergy, nothing individually load bearing. Resilient and grindy, with a capped ceiling. The Twinning lives here, and so does the Rhystic slop meta at large.

LEGACY JESKAI CONTROL: THE ARMOR

The constructed form in which I tried to actually come from,. Legacy's WUR control shells: Swords, dense cheap countermagic, maindeck Pyroblast culture, and winning on card quality plus inevitability. (yup it has it all) KeepSeven's Jeskai Control tournament report on Eternal Durdles is the reference I keep coming back to. My Rhystic, Sentinel, Remora, and hatebear layer is that lineage in 99 card form. It is the armor, not the sword. The pointed inheritance note: Legacy Jeskai runs on Brainstorm and Ponder velocity, the one organ I refused to grow for ten months.

STICKY JENGA (the target)

Fast and consistent and resilient, where the resilience is structural instead of borrowed from card quality (v1) or lockout (v2). Shared blocks across routes, tutors and clones as spare parts, recursion as a second act, and an outlet in the command zone that nobody can remove. A tower that stays up while blocks are being pulled.

Finding 1: my Bribery tech I realized was DNA, not typical jinfusion jank. All three roster decks win through Consult and Oracle in black. Gwen is my only deck without black, and I rebuilt my home win axis anyway. Loop to an empty library, then Bribery the table's Oracle. Comparing my own decks validated what outside comparison called a pet card.

Finding 2: my cantrip blind spot has a root cause. Zero cheap cantrips in Scion. Zero in Terra. Top only in Jin. I buy consistency with black tutor density, which is correct in black. I carried that heuristic into the one deck whose colors can't match black's tutor quality, then under-compensated with selection for ten months. Not a Gwen bug. A habit of mine meeting the one deck it doesn't fit. And the Legacy Jeskai lineage this deck claims is defined by the Brainstorm and Ponder engine. The ancestor demands the organ I skipped.

Finding 3: the ritual gap, same story. Every black deck I run has five or six rituals. Gwen runs two burst spells while wanting turbo pacing. My next experiment queue: one or two red rituals (both lived in Vadrok) versus one more cantrip. Testable, cheap, reversible.

The three-pillar audit

PILLAR 1 · SELF

Done above. My constants identified, two divergences (selection, rituals) named with root causes, and one former "pet card" reclassified as DNA.

PILLAR 2 · ARCHETYPE

The commander dictates the axis. Gwen's is the exile engine, and it stays primary. The warning I take from my own roster: over-importing Scion's habits builds a worse Scion, not a better Gwen. The Oracle heist stays backup, never the plan.

PILLAR 3 · META

The asymmetry I have to respect: all three of my black decks run Orcish Bowmasters. Gwen can't, and fields my softest board against it (Gwen 2/1, Sentinel, Ragavan, Spellseeker). Sequencing discipline in Bowmasters pods is my reps priority, and it is Solitude's strongest argument.

Part V

The Math

Hypergeometric analysis across all five lists, with formulas shown, work shown, and my honest reading of what moved and what didn't.

The base formula

Every probability below is drawn without replacement from a 99 card deck. For a category of K relevant cards seen over n draws:

P(X ≥ 1) = 1 − C(N−K, n) / C(N, n) N = 99 Worked example, my final list, ≥1 interaction in the opener (K=19, n=7): P = 1 − C(80,7)/C(99,7) = 1 − 3,176,716,400 / 15,094,376,275 = 1 − 0.2105 = 0.787 → 78.7%

My honest finding, amended: raw assembly sat flat for ten months. 19.4% in v1, 19.4% in v3.1. Structure improved while my draw odds didn't.
